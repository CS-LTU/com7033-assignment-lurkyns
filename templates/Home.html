What is a Stroke?

A stroke occurs when blood flow to a part of the brain is interrupted or reduced, preventing brain tissue from getting oxygen and nutrients. This can lead to brain cell damage or death. Strokes are a medical emergency, and prompt treatment is crucial to reduce long-term effects.

Causes

Strokes can be caused by blocked arteries (ischemic stroke) or the leaking or bursting of blood vessels (hemorrhagic stroke). Temporary disruption of blood flow to the brain, known as a transient ischemic attack (TIA), can also lead to a stroke.

Types of Stroke

  • Ischemic Stroke: Caused by a blood clot blocking a blood vessel in the brain.
  • Hemorrhagic Stroke: Caused by a blood vessel bursting in the brain.
  • TIA (Mini-Stroke): A temporary blockage that doesn't cause lasting damage.

Prevention

You can reduce the risk of a stroke by maintaining a healthy lifestyle:

  • Eat a balanced diet low in salt, fat, and sugar.
  • Exercise regularly to maintain a healthy weight.
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption.
  • Manage conditions like high blood pressure, diabetes, and cholesterol.
